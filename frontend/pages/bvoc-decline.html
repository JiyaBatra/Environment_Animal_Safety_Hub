BVOC Compounds

Biogenic volatile organic compounds include isoprene, monoterpenes, and sesquiterpenes emitted by plants. These compounds are crucial for atmospheric chemistry and climate regulation.

Atmospheric Chemistry

BVOCs react with NOx and OH radicals to form ozone and secondary organic aerosols. These processes influence air quality, cloud formation, and radiative forcing.

Climate Feedbacks

Reduced BVOC emissions in degraded forests can decrease cloud formation and aerosol cooling effects, potentially leading to regional warming and altered precipitation patterns.

Mitigation Strategies

  • Implement forest restoration programs
  • Reduce deforestation and degradation
  • Monitor BVOC emissions in vulnerable areas
  • Develop climate-resilient tree species
  • Integrate BVOC dynamics into climate models
