Tree Hydraulic Stress Mechanisms

×

Xylem Cavitation

Drought-induced air bubble formation in water-conducting vessels reduces hydraulic conductivity and causes embolism.

Stomatal Closure

Trees close leaf pores to conserve water, reducing photosynthesis and increasing oxidative stress.

Root Function Decline

Reduced root hydraulic conductivity limits water uptake and nutrient transport to aerial tissues.

Tree Defense Responses

×

Constitutive Defenses

Pre-formed physical and chemical barriers that provide immediate protection against pathogens.

Induced Defenses

Activated defense responses triggered by pathogen recognition, including phytoalexin production.

Systemic Resistance

Whole-tree defense signaling that prepares undamaged tissues for potential pathogen attack.

Fungal Pathogen Ecology

×

Endophytic Phase

Many fungi live harmlessly inside trees until stress conditions trigger pathogenic behavior.

Opportunistic Pathogens

Saprophytic fungi that switch to parasitic lifestyles when trees become weakened by drought.

Pathogen Complexes

Multiple fungal species often work together, with primary pathogens creating entry points for secondary invaders.

Fungal Outbreak Dynamics

×

Initiation Phase

Drought stress creates susceptible hosts, allowing latent pathogens to become active.

Expansion Phase

Successful infections produce spores that spread to neighboring stressed trees.

Climax Phase

Massive tree mortality creates ideal conditions for saprophytic fungi and secondary pathogens.
