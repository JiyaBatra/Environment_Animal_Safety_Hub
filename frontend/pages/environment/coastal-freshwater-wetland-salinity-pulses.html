Coastal Freshwater Wetland Salinity Pulses

Analyzing episodic saltwater intrusion impacts on vital wetland ecosystems

Understanding Salinity Pulses

Coastal freshwater wetlands face increasing threats from episodic saltwater intrusion events. These "salinity pulses" occur when storm surges, sea-level rise, or reduced freshwater inflow allow seawater to penetrate freshwater ecosystems.

The impacts are devastating, causing rapid changes in plant communities, soil chemistry, and wildlife populations. Understanding these pulses is crucial for developing effective conservation strategies.

Ecological Consequences

Salinity pulses trigger cascading effects throughout wetland ecosystems:

  • Plant Dieback: Freshwater species cannot tolerate sudden salinity increases
  • Soil Degradation: Sodium accumulation alters soil structure and fertility
  • Wildlife Displacement: Fish, amphibians, and birds lose critical habitats
  • Carbon Loss: Wetlands' ability to sequester carbon diminishes
