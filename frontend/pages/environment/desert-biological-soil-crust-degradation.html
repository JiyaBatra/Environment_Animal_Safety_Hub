The Hidden Foundation of Desert Ecosystems

Biological soil crusts (biocrusts) are complex communities of cyanobacteria, lichens, mosses, and fungi that form a living skin on desert soils. These remarkable organisms play crucial roles in carbon and nitrogen fixation, soil stabilization, and water retention in arid ecosystems worldwide.

ü¶† Microbial Communities

Cyanobacteria, lichens, and fungi form symbiotic relationships that fix atmospheric nitrogen and carbon.

üå± Soil Stabilization

Crusts prevent erosion by binding soil particles and creating surface roughness that traps wind-blown sediments.

üíß Water Dynamics

Crusts influence water infiltration, evaporation, and runoff patterns in desert environments.

Current Crisis

Human activities and climate change are causing widespread degradation of biocrusts, leading to significant losses in ecosystem services and biodiversity.

Off-road vehicle activity has destroyed 40-60% of biocrusts. Recovery takes 50-100 years.

Grazing and climate change have reduced nitrogen fixation by 70% in some areas.

Sahara Desert, Africa
