The Crisis of Polar Primary Production

Ice-dependent algal blooms represent one of the most critical, yet least understood, components of polar marine ecosystems. These microscopic algae, primarily diatoms, colonize the underside of sea ice during extended winter darkness, forming the foundation of Arctic and Antarctic food webs.

Scientific Background

Algal Ecology

Sea ice provides a stable substrate where algae can access both light and nutrients. The ice matrix protects cells from wind mixing while allowing sufficient light penetration for photosynthesis.

Nutrient Dynamics

Upwelling through leads and polynyas delivers nutrient-rich deep water. Blooms are fueled by nitrate, phosphate, and trace metals like iron from atmospheric deposition.

Seasonal Timing

Blooms initiate in early winter darkness and grow throughout the ice-covered period. Spring melt releases algae into the water column, seeding the pelagic bloom.
