Geomorphological Alteration

Sand mining removes sediment from riverbeds, causing incision (downcutting) that alters channel morphology, increases flow velocity, and triggers upstream erosion.

Aquatic Habitat Loss

Riverbed incision destroys spawning grounds, disrupts pool-riffle sequences, and alters hyporheic zones, leading to significant biodiversity decline.

Incision Mechanisms

Direct sediment removal creates a sediment deficit, increasing erosive power and initiating a feedback loop of deepening and widening.

Mitigation Strategies

  • Implement mining limits based on sediment transport capacity
  • Use selective mining techniques to preserve morphology
  • Construct check dams and grade control structures
  • Promote alternative sand sources (recycled, offshore)
  • Establish long-term monitoring programs
