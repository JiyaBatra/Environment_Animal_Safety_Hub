Marine Larval Orientation Loss from Acidification

Examining sensory impairment in early life stages and its impact on marine ecosystems

Overview

Ocean acidification is disrupting the sensory abilities of marine larvae, particularly their orientation and navigation capabilities. This impairment affects their ability to find suitable habitats, avoid predators, and locate food sources during critical early life stages.

Sensory Impairment Mechanisms

Olfactory Disruption

Acidification interferes with chemosensory receptors, impairing the detection of chemical cues used for navigation and settlement.

Statocyst Damage

Changes in pH affect the statocysts (balance organs) in larvae, disrupting their ability to maintain orientation in water currents.

Neural Development

Altered pH levels during development affect the formation and function of sensory neurons and neural pathways.

Calcium Carbonate Effects

Reduced availability of carbonate ions affects the calcification of sensory structures and otoliths.

Ecological and Population Impacts

Settlement Failure

Larvae fail to locate and settle in appropriate habitats, leading to higher mortality rates.

Population Declines

Reduced recruitment success affects adult populations and ecosystem structure.

Food Web Disruption

Changes in larval distribution affect predator-prey relationships and energy flow.
