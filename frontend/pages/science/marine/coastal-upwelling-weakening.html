Scientific Background

Coastal Upwelling Mechanism

Wind-driven surface currents move offshore, allowing nutrient-rich deep waters to rise and replace them. This brings essential nutrients from depths of 100-500 meters to the sunlit surface layer.

  • Ekman Transport: Wind stress causes surface water movement
  • Nutrient Enrichment: Deep waters contain 10-100x higher nutrients
  • Seasonal Patterns: Most pronounced during spring/summer

Weakening Mechanisms

Climate change alters conditions driving upwelling through wind pattern changes, ocean circulation modifications, and increased stratification.

  • Reduced Wind Stress: Global warming weakens pressure gradients
  • Stratification Increase: Warmer waters create stronger density gradients
  • Current Modifications: Altered ocean currents reduce favorable conditions

Regional Manifestations

California Current

Upwelling indices show 20-30% decline since 1970s, affecting sardine and anchovy fisheries.

Humboldt Current

Peruvian upwelling reduced by 15-25% in recent decades, impacting global fishmeal production.

Canary Current

Northwest African upwelling weakening affects Moroccan fisheries and coastal communities.
