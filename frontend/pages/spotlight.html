Amur Leopard

Panthera pardus orientalis
100+ Population
Critically Endangered
Russia/China Range

The Amur leopard is one of the rarest big cats on Earth. With its beautiful spotted coat and incredible agility, it's a symbol of the wild forests of eastern Russia and northern China. But these magnificent creatures are fighting for survival.

Size & Weight

Length: 2-3 meters (including tail)

Weight: 25-75 kg

Height: 50-70 cm at shoulder

Lifespan

Wild: 10-15 years

Captivity: Up to 20 years

Sexual Maturity: 2-3 years

Diet

Primary: Deer, wild boar

Secondary: Hares, badgers, mice

Hunting: Solitary, nocturnal

Threat Level

CRITICAL
Vulnerable Endangered Critically Endangered Extinct in Wild

Major Threats

Poaching

Hunted for their beautiful spotted coats and body parts used in traditional medicine. Despite protection, poaching remains a serious threat.

Habitat Loss

Forests are cleared for agriculture, development, and logging. Their territory has shrunk by over 80% in the past century.

Inbreeding

With such a small population, genetic diversity is extremely low, making them vulnerable to diseases and birth defects.

Road Kill

New roads through their habitat increase the risk of vehicle collisions, especially when they cross territories.

Conservation Efforts

Protected Areas

Landzou Nature Reserve in Russia protects about 60% of remaining habitat. Anti-poaching teams patrol regularly.

Conservation Timeline

1970

Population Crash

Only 30 individuals estimated left in the wild due to heavy poaching.

1998

Landzou Reserve Created

Russian government establishes 250,000 hectare protected area.

2007

Camera Trap Success

First camera trap photos confirm breeding population.

2015

Population Doubles

Estimated 60-80 individuals in the wild.

2025

Hope on the Horizon

Population exceeds 100 for first time in 50 years.
